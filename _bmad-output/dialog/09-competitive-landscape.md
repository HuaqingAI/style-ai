## Alternative Categories

### 1. Do Nothing / Continue Trial-and-Error

Users continue buying, trying hairstyles, asking friends, listening to salespeople or hairstylists, or avoiding change entirely.

**Why users stick with it:** Zero setup, no privacy risk, no new app to learn.

**Where it falls short:** Users still cannot see "what this looks like on me" before spending money or making a visible change.

### 2. Content Platforms and Ecommerce References

Xiaohongshu, Douyin, ecommerce model photos, and buyer reviews provide inspiration and social proof.

**Why users stick with it:** Huge content supply, habit, social proof, shopping context.

**Where it falls short:** These show other people's effects, not the user's own effect.

### 3. Direct AI Try-On / Try-Style Competitors

Google Shopping virtual try-on, Google Doppl, Lookie, Meitu-like tools, and other AI try-on apps can generate try-on outputs from user photos or outfit images.

**Why users stick with it:** Platform scale, mature AI investment, shopping/content integration, potentially lower friction.

**Where it falls short for Style AI's target:** Many are still tool-like or platform-specific; they may not focus on daily beauty beginners who need recommendation, avoid guidance, and cross-category personal fit analysis.

### 4. Hairstyle / Makeup Virtual Try-On Tools

YouCam Makeup / Perfect Corp and similar beauty tools have deep category experience in AR and generative hairstyle or makeup try-on.

**Why users stick with it:** Category depth, established beauty use cases, mature virtual try-on expectations.

**Where it falls short:** Often category-specific, while Style AI aims to combine hairstyle, outfit, accessory, makeup, and personal fit explanation under one beginner-oriented flow.

### 5. General AI Image Tools

OpenAI image generation and similar model tools can generate or edit images.

**Why users stick with it:** Powerful model capability and flexible prompts.

**Where it falls short:** Ordinary users still face prompt, workflow, environment, and judgment barriers, especially in domestic contexts.

## Unfair Advantage Hypothesis

Style AI should not claim its core advantage is simply "more realistic generation"; this will be copied and commoditized quickly.

The MVP advantage is narrower:

- More focused on Chinese daily beauty beginners.
- Converts outside inspiration into "my own effect".
- Combines realistic preview with recommendation, avoid guidance, and plain-language reasoning.
- Uses a dual-entry structure: personal image generation for first-time value, universal try-on / try-style for shopping and content-triggered moments.
- Builds around generation credits and seed-user feedback from the start.

## Reality Check

If Google Doppl, Lookie, Meitu-like tools, or ecommerce platforms add personal image analysis reports, Style AI loses any generic try-on advantage.

The remaining defendable angle is not technology alone, but product focus: beginner-friendly flow, Chinese style categories, practical recommendation logic, low-friction logged-in usage, optional personal profile, and fast iteration based on seed users.
